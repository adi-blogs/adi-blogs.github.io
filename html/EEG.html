What is the sleep cycle (EEGs)

EEG, or electroencephalography, is a crucial measurement technique in the study of sleep patterns and brain activity during different sleep stages. It involves placing electrodes on the scalp to record electrical brain activity. Sleep is a complex process with distinct stages, including REM (rapid eye movement) and non-REM phases, each associated with specific EEG patterns. As an individual transitions from wakefulness to sleep, their brain exhibits alpha and theta waves, indicating relaxation and drowsiness. Deeper sleep stages are characterized by slower delta waves. Conversely, REM sleep, where vivid dreaming occurs, displays beta-like brainwave patterns resembling wakefulness. When a patient with insomnia seeks evaluation, EEG can reveal irregularities in sleep architecture, aiding in diagnosis and the development of tailored treatment strategies. It helps uncover the intricate interplay of sleep stages in memory consolidation, emotional processing, and cognitive restoration, contributing to a deeper understanding of the role of sleep in maintaining overall well-being.
