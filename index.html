This is a salamander!

Lizards possess dry and scaly skin, in contrast to salamanders, which have moist and porous skin. Similar to humans, all lizards rely on lungs for breathing. Salamanders, however, exhibit a diverse range of respiratory adaptations. They can breathe through their skin, utilize gills, employ lungs, or in certain instances, employ a combination of both skin and lungs for respiration. (It Is a Salamander. No, It Is a Lizard.
