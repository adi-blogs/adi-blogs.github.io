Insomnia is a common sleep disorder that affects millions of people worldwide. It's characterized by dissatifcation in falling asleep, staying asleep, or both. Insomnia can be acute (short-term) or chronic (long-term), and it can have a variety of causes.

source

Prevalence

According to Levenson, Kay, & Buysse (2015), prevalence rates of insomnia disorder is set between at 5% to 15% of people worldwide. It is the second-most common mental disorder. This is often diagnosed by questioning “Do you experience difficulty sleeping?” or “Do you have difficulty staying asleep?” These questions on insomina can be seen as a way to clue into what underlying cause may be at play. Other objective measures like actigraphy or polysomnography are not (yet) part of the routine diagnoses.

Models and Solutions

Perpetuating Factors The “3P model,”110 a diathesis-stress model, describes a set of predisposing, precipitating, and perpetuating factors that may contribute to the development and maintenance of insomnia. Predisposing factors, such as age or sex, make an individual more susceptible to insomnia, and precipitating factors are events that coincide with the onset of insomnia, such as major stressors.
Increasing time in bed to “catch up” on sleep doesn't work if it isn't consistent.

Stimulus Control
"In 1972, Bootzin112 proposed that stimuli associated with sleep (eg, a quiet, dark bedroom) become discriminative stimuli that reinforce sleep."
Try to sleep in the same dark and quiet room

Cognitive Model
"The cognitive model of insomnia25 proposes that individuals with insomnia are susceptible to excessive worry and unpleasant intrusive thoughts, particularly those related to getting enough sleep and the consequences of sleep disturbance. This worry may develop into sleep-related anxiety, lead to increased vigilance for sleep-related threats (eg, watching the clock at night), and ultimately result in an exaggeration of the magnitude of the actual sleep disruption. Cognitive therapy for insomnia challenges these maladaptive cognitive processes and limits the behaviors that maintain unhelpful beliefs and insomnia.113"
Calm your mind before you sleep
